Vista allows you to connect multiple TV blocks together to create large, multi-block screens. This guide will walk you through the mechanics of creating impressive TV walls.

## Understanding TV Connections

TV blocks automatically detect and connect to adjacent TV blocks that are facing the same direction. The mod uses a sophisticated grid system to form rectangular screens.

<Note>
The maximum size of connected TVs is configurable in `vista-common.toml`. By default, TV walls can be up to 8×8 blocks, but this can be adjusted from 1 to 32 blocks per side.
</Note>

## Creating a Multi-Block TV

<Steps>
  <Step title="Place Your First TV Block">
    Start by placing a TV block facing the direction you want your screen to face. This will be part of your multi-block structure.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Adjacent TV Blocks">
    Place additional TV blocks next to your first block. They must:
    - Face the **same direction** as the first block
    - Be directly adjacent (horizontally or vertically)
    - Form a rectangular shape

    The TVs will automatically connect and form a larger screen.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Avoid Manual Connection Mode">
    By default, TV blocks automatically expand their connections when placed. If you want to prevent automatic connection, **hold Shift** while placing the block.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Power Your TV Wall">
    Apply redstone power to any TV block in the connected structure. The entire TV wall will turn on together.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connection Types

The mod uses 10 different connection states defined in `TVType.java:11-21`:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Single TV" icon="square">
    A standalone TV block with no connections.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Corner Connections" icon="vector-square">
    - **TOP_LEFT**: Connected to the right and below
    - **TOP_RIGHT**: Connected to the left and below
    - **BOTTOM_LEFT**: Connected to the right and above
    - **BOTTOM_RIGHT**: Connected to the left and above
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Edge Connections" icon="minus">
    - **TOP**: Connected to left, right, and below
    - **BOTTOM**: Connected to left, right, and above
    - **LEFT**: Connected to top, bottom, and right
    - **RIGHT**: Connected to top, bottom, and left
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Center Connection" icon="grid">
    - **CENTER**: Connected on all four sides (top, bottom, left, right)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How Connection Detection Works

When you place a TV block, Vista's `RectFinder` algorithm (`RectFinder.java:76-116`) searches for the largest possible rectangular configuration:

1. **Initial Detection**: The block checks all four adjacent positions for compatible TV blocks
2. **Rectangle Expansion**: The algorithm attempts to expand in all directions to form the largest rectangle
3. **Master Block Selection**: The bottom-left TV in the grid becomes the "master" that stores the cassette
4. **Connection Update**: All TV blocks update their connection state to reflect their position in the grid

<Tip>
The master block (bottom-left corner) is the only one that can hold a cassette. When you interact with any TV in the wall, you're actually interacting with the master block.
</Tip>

## Grid System

The connection system uses a grid-based approach defined in `TVBlock.java:271-354`:

```java
// From TVBlock.java:282-286
public TVGridAccess(Level level, BlockPos pos, BlockState state) {
    this.pos = pos;
    this.facing = state.getValue(FACING);
    this.level = level;
}
```

Each TV stores its:
- **Connection type**: Which edges connect to other TVs
- **Power state**: Whether it's receiving redstone power
- **Master reference**: Location of the bottom-left TV (which holds the cassette)

## Breaking and Reshaping

When you break a TV block from a multi-block structure:

1. The algorithm finds the closest remaining TV to the center
2. That TV becomes the new master block
3. The remaining TVs reconnect to form the largest possible rectangle
4. If the old master held a cassette, it transfers to the new master

<Warning>
If you break the master block (bottom-left corner), the cassette will automatically transfer to the new master block. No items are lost!
</Warning>

## Screen Rendering

The screen size is calculated based on the number of connected blocks (`TVBlockEntity.java:250-256`):

```java
public int getScreenPixelWidth() {
    return Math.max(1, connectedTvsAmount) * 16 - EDGE_PIXEL_LEN;
}

public int getScreenPixelHeight() {
    return Math.max(1, connectedTvsAmount) * 16 - EDGE_PIXEL_LEN;
}
```

Each TV block contributes 16 pixels (minus 4 pixels for the border), so a 3×3 TV wall creates a 44×44 pixel screen.

## Tips for Large TV Walls

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Square Configurations" icon="square">
    Square configurations (2×2, 3×3, 4×4) work best for most content. The algorithm prioritizes square shapes when possible.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Cinema Achievement" icon="trophy">
    Place a cassette in a TV wall of 3 or more connected blocks while powered to earn the "Cinema" advancement!
  </Card>

  <Card title="Power Efficiency" icon="bolt">
    You only need to power one TV block in the structure. The power state propagates to all connected TVs.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Same Direction Required" icon="compass">
    All TVs must face the exact same direction. Even a single misaligned TV will break the connection.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="TVs Won't Connect" icon="unlink">
    Check that:
    - All TVs are facing the same direction
    - The shape forms a perfect rectangle
    - You haven't exceeded the max size configured in `vista-common.toml`
    - You're not holding Shift while placing (which prevents auto-connection)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can't Insert Cassette" icon="cassette-tape">
    You must interact with the master block (bottom-left corner). Try clicking different TVs in the wall until you find it, or break and replace to reset the master.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Different Content on Different TVs" icon="clone">
    This shouldn't happen if properly connected. If it does, try breaking and replacing the TVs to force a reconnection.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
